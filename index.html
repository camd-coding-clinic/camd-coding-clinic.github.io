Do you need coding help?

The Coding Clinic is here to support you! Meet with expert students to get support and resources around everything related to coding and computation: troubleshooting problems, resolving errors, strategizing solutions, and more. We can help you with questions such as:

  • Do you need help understanding the fundamentals of programming, or the mechanics of a specific programming language?
  • Are you confused about how your IDE works?
  • Is something stuck in your code, and you don’t know how to fix it?
  • Do you have a design or technical specification for a project, but you don’t know how to translate it into functional code?
  • Did you build a project, but want feedback on its technical design or functionality?

Expert students are available to help with these questions and more. We’re happy to point you in the right direction on all things coding!

3 ways to get help

Expert students are available to support you Monday through Friday:

  • By Appointment In-person consultations: Book an appointment with Clinic staff during regular Clinic hours (below) in Ryder 236 for consultation on coding problems
  • By Appointment Remote consultations: Limited appointments are also available for consultations outside of regular Clinic hours, over Zoom, with Clinic staff depending on staff availability
  • Online NEW: Support on Discord: Coding Clinic and Tools Clinic have teamed up to offer a Discord server, Art + Design Clinics, where you can ask questions about coding and computation, design tools and software, and everything in between. The server is managed by Clinics staff, who can help answer questions you might have. In addition, users who join may offer peer support, beyond Clinics staff, based on availability. Join the Art + Design Clinics server on Discord here!

Please note the following:

  • In-person consultation: Coding Clinic staff are available by appointment to meet with you and provide support during regular Clinic open hours. Outside of pre-booked appointments, additional support is available during regular Clinic hours on a drop-in basis, first come, first served; booked consultations take priority. Book and appointment today to talk to Clinic staff!
  • Virtual consultation: Coding Clinic staff may be available for virtual consultations, during regular Clinic hours, depending on availability; use the booking form to check availability.
  • Reserving Coding Clinic space: Outside of regular open Clinic hours, the Coding Clinic space is open for reservation on Tuesdays for activities related to code and computation. Space is reservable on the following conditions:
    • Coding Clinic space reservations are first come, first served and must be requested at least 72 hours in advance
    • Reservation requests must be approved by the Clinic before space may be used
    • Space may only be reserved by groups of at least 4 people and no more than 10 people
    • Space may only be used for work on projects or activities related to coding or computation, e.g., project work, small workshops and demos, peer troubleshooting and support, course-related functions, etc.
    • When reserving the space, you will be required to provide names and contact information of all group members, along with a description of how the space will be used

Support for faculty

If you are a faculty member teaching a course that involves a coding or computational component, you may utilize Coding Clinic for the following:

  • Collaborations to support pedagogy: If you are teaching a course that involves a computational component and you have specific concerns or ideas related to teaching that you want to discuss, you may reach out to Steven Geofrey to arrange a consultation. Depending on use case, Coding Clinic may be able to arrange collaborations or interventions specifically tied to your course. Examples of past arrangements include:
    • Setting up a course assignment requiring a consultation with Coding Clinic staff, based around a course project or assignment, and verifying attendance
    • Tracking and monitoring what questions students in your course bring to Coding Clinic consultations
    • Developing a list of resources that may support student learning in your course
  • Reserved space for faculty or courses: Faculty may reserve the Coding Clinic space for specific course-related needs, as long as they have a clear computational or coding component (e.g., holding a project meeting with a student group; setting up a support session led by a TA). Please note:
    • Coding Clinic space may only be reserved for faculty or course-specific needs in isolated sessions, or in short series of sessions; you cannot reserve the space for long or repeated durations (e.g., you cannot reserve every Monday for a course need)
    • All reservation requests must go through and be approved by Steven Geofrey; significant reservation requests such as small workshops should be made at least 1 week in advance of the requested date
  • Workshops, demonstrations, and exhibitions: The space may be reserved for short-form workshops on special topics, demonstrations of specific projects, tools, or resources, and exhibitions of student work. Events may be initiated and organized by Coding Clinic staff, or faculty and student groups may also initiate events. Reach out to Steven Geofrey to discuss possibilities and make arrangements to reserve the space.
